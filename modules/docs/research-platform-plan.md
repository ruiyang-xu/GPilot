## 1. Platform Vision

Build the most credible solo-GP research voice in cross-border venture by combining four strengths stolen from the benchmarks:

| Source | What We Steal | How We Apply It |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Goldman Sachs** | Data-visualization-first, proprietary frameworks, quantitative rigor | Every piece anchored by original charts and data tables. Build signature frameworks (e.g., "Unicorn Cost Curve") |
| **晚点LatePost** | Narrative depth, insider access, investigative credibility | Long-form company teardowns with founder/operator quotes and non-obvious angles |
| **海外独角兽** | Information asymmetry bridging, WeChat-native formatting, consistent cadence | Chinese-language coverage of overseas markets that Chinese LPs/founders can't get elsewhere |
| **AB Bernstein** | Contrarian thematic positioning, "industry analysts from within" credibility | GP-as-practitioner voice — writing from the investor's seat, not the journalist's desk |

**Core differentiator**: A practicing investor who publishes institutional-grade research in two languages, with real portfolio data informing the analysis (anonymized). No one else does this at the intersection of EN/CN, VC practitioner, and public thought leadership.

### 2.2 Series Details

---

#### Series A: Sector Deep Dives 「赛道纵深」

**Frequency**: Monthly (12/year)
**Length**: 3,000–5,000 words | Bilingual (publish EN + CN versions)
**Comparable**: Goldman Sachs "Top of Mind" + a16z thematic research

**Structure**:
```
1. Thesis Statement (1 paragraph — the contrarian claim)
2. Market Map (visual: landscape diagram of the sector)
3. Why Now (macro/tech/regulatory catalysts — data-heavy)
4. Value Chain Analysis (where value accrues, with framework chart)
5. Company Profiles (3–5 companies representing different positions)
6. Investment Implications (what this means for capital allocation)
7. Key Data Appendix (charts, tables, comps)
```

**Quality Standards**:
- Minimum 5 original charts/visualizations per piece
- At least 1 proprietary framework or matrix per piece
- Primary data where possible (survey, interviews, proprietary analysis)
- Explicit investment thesis with conviction level (High/Medium/Low)
- All claims cited or attributed

**Example Topics**:
- "AI Agent Infrastructure: The $200B Middleware Layer" (AI)
- "Vertical SaaS in Emerging Markets: Why India ≠ US SaaS Playbook" (SaaS)
- "The New Space Economy: From Launch to On-Orbit Services" (Deep Tech)
- "Cross-Border Fintech: Stablecoin Rails Replacing SWIFT" (Fintech)
- "Synthetic Biology Platforms: Drug Discovery to Food Systems" (Biotech)

---

#### Series B: Company Teardowns 「独角兽拆解」

**Frequency**: Bi-weekly (24/year)
**Length**: 2,000–3,000 words | Bilingual
**Comparable**: 晚点LatePost investigative analysis + GS initiation coverage

**Structure**:
```
1. Company Snapshot (1-page visual: key stats, funding, valuation, team)
2. The Business (what they do, for whom, how they make money)
3. Moat Analysis (network effects, switching costs, data, scale, brand)
4. Financial Deep Dive (revenue model, unit economics, growth trajectory)
5. Competitive Landscape (market map with positioning)
6. Team & Culture (founders, key hires, Glassdoor/culture signals)
7. Bull Case / Bear Case (explicit, balanced)
8. GP Verdict (invest/watch/pass — with reasoning)
```

**Quality Standards**:
- Financial model or unit economics table for every company
- Competitive positioning chart (2x2 or landscape)
- At least 2 non-obvious insights (not available from a quick Google)
- Founder/operator quotes where available (from public interviews or personal conversations)
- Historical context: funding rounds, pivots, key decisions

**Example Companies**:
- Anthropic (AI infrastructure)
- Stripe (fintech/payments)
- SpaceX (space/defense)
- 小红书/Xiaohongshu (consumer/social)
- Wiz (cybersecurity)

---

#### Series C: Market Pulse 「市场脉搏」

**Frequency**: Weekly (50/year)
**Length**: 800–1,500 words | Bilingual
**Comparable**: 海外独角兽 curated briefing + The Information deal log

**Structure**:
```
1. This Week's Signal (1 key theme or trend — 2-3 sentences)
2. Top Deals (3-5 notable funding rounds with 1-line commentary)
3. Market Data (chart: sector heat map, valuation trends, or exit activity)
4. Deep Read (1 recommended article/report with GP commentary)
5. Portfolio Pulse (anonymized — 1 metric or milestone from portfolio)
6. One Question (thought-provoking question for reader engagement)
```

**Quality Standards**:
- Published every Tuesday (CN) and Wednesday (EN)
- Must contain at least 1 original data visualization
- Deal commentary goes beyond "Company X raised $Y" — explain why it matters
- "Portfolio Pulse" section builds credibility as a practitioner (anonymized)
- Total reading time: 3–5 minutes

**WeChat-Specific Formatting**:
- Title: ≤31 characters for preview visibility
- 17px body text, mobile-first layout
- Visual break every 150 words
- 1 image per 300 words minimum
- Emoji section headers for scanability

---

#### Series D: GP Insight Letters 「GP洞见」

**Frequency**: Quarterly (4/year)
**Length**: 1,500–2,500 words | Bilingual
**Comparable**: AB Bernstein contrarian thematic + Howard Marks memos + Sequoia "Black Swans"

**Structure**:
```
1. Opening Provocation (a bold, specific claim or question)
2. The Conventional Wisdom (what most people believe)
3. Why It's Wrong / Incomplete (data + reasoning)
4. Framework (introduce a mental model or decision framework)
5. Implications for Founders & Investors
6. What We're Doing About It (portfolio-informed, anonymized)
```

**Quality Standards**:
- Must contain at least 1 original framework or concept
- Contrarian but substantiated — not contrarian for shock value
- Written in first person, GP voice (not institutional "we")
- Should be quotable — at least 2-3 sentences worthy of being shared standalone
- No more than 1 chart — this series is about ideas, not data

**Example Topics**:
- "The Valuation Paradox: Why Cheap Rounds Create Expensive Problems"
- "On Governance: What I Learned Sitting on 12 Boards as a Solo GP"
- "The China Premium is Back — Here's How to Think About It"
- "Speed as Strategy: Why 72-Hour Term Sheets Win in 2026"

## 3. Editorial Workflow (Cowork-Powered)

### 3.1 Research Production Pipeline

```
┌──────────┐     ┌──────────┐     ┌──────────┐     ┌──────────┐     ┌──────────┐
│  IDEATE  │────▶│ RESEARCH │────▶│  DRAFT   │────▶│  DESIGN  │────▶│ PUBLISH  │
│          │     │          │     │          │     │          │     │          │
│ GP picks │     │ Cowork   │     │ Cowork   │     │ Cowork   │     │ GP posts │
│ topic &  │     │ gathers  │     │ drafts   │     │ formats  │     │ to WeChat│
│ angle    │     │ data &   │     │ full     │     │ HTML for │     │ + LinkedIn│
│          │     │ sources  │     │ article  │     │ both     │     │          │
│          │     │          │     │          │     │ platforms│     │          │
└──────────┘     └──────────┘     └──────────┘     └──────────┘     └──────────┘
     │                │                │                │                │
     ▼                ▼                ▼                ▼                ▼
  GP input        Web search       CN + EN          WeChat HTML      Schedule
  + thesis        Company data     versions         LinkedIn MD      via Cowork
  direction       Market comps     GP reviews       Charts/visuals   reminder
                  Public filings   & edits          Mobile-first
```

### 3.2 Step-by-Step Workflow Per Publication

**Step 1: Topic Selection & Brief** (GP — 10 min)
- GP provides: topic, angle, key thesis, target companies/data points
- Cowork generates a research brief with suggested sources and structure

**Step 2: Data Gathering** (Cowork — automated)
- Web search for recent news, funding data, public filings
- Competitive landscape mapping
- Pull relevant data from deal tracker and portfolio dashboard
- Compile source list with key quotes and statistics

**Step 3: First Draft** (Cowork drafts, GP reviews)
- Cowork writes full draft following series-specific template
- Generates both CN and EN versions
- Creates placeholder descriptions for charts/visualizations
- GP reviews, edits for voice and proprietary insights (30–60 min)

**Step 4: Visualization & Design** (Cowork — automated)
- Generates HTML charts using Recharts/D3 components
- Creates WeChat-optimized HTML article with proper formatting
- Creates LinkedIn-optimized markdown/article version
- Generates social media preview cards

**Step 5: Quality Check** (Cowork — automated)
- Fact-checks claims against sources
- Verifies data accuracy in charts
- Checks mobile rendering for WeChat
- Runs readability score (target: educated professional level)
- Flags any unsourced claims

**Step 6: Publication** (GP — 10 min)
- GP does final review and posts to WeChat公众号
- GP posts LinkedIn version
- Cowork drafts social media teasers for cross-promotion

## 6. Quality Standards & Differentiation

### 6.1 Quality Checklist (Every Publication)

```
BEFORE PUBLISHING — MANDATORY CHECKS

□ Thesis is clear and stated in first 100 words
□ At least 1 non-obvious insight (not available from a quick search)
□ All data claims sourced or attributed
□ Charts are accurate, labeled, and sourced
□ CN and EN versions are culturally localized (not just translated)
□ Mobile rendering verified for WeChat version
□ Reading time is within target range for the series
□ GP has reviewed and added proprietary perspective
□ No confidential portfolio data exposed (anonymized where used)
□ Conclusion includes clear takeaway for the reader
```

### 6.2 What Makes This Better Than the Benchmarks

| vs. Benchmark | Our Advantage |
|--------------|---------------|
| **vs. Goldman Sachs** | We publish openly (they're behind a paywall). We write for founders AND investors (they write only for institutional buyers). We have practitioner insight from portfolio companies. |
| **vs. 晚点LatePost** | We add investment frameworks and financial analysis to narrative (they're journalists, not investors). We cover global markets (they focus on China tech). |
| **vs. 海外独角兽** | We go deeper — full financial models and competitive analysis, not just news curation. We publish in both EN and CN (they're CN-only). We have proprietary portfolio data. |
| **vs. AB Bernstein** | We cover private markets (they focus on public equities). We're bilingual. We're accessible to founders, not just institutional subscribers. We move faster (weekly vs. quarterly). |

### 6.3 Credibility Building Tactics

1. **Show your work**: Include data tables, cite sources, link to primary documents
2. **Practitioner voice**: Reference anonymized portfolio experience ("In our experience across 3 portfolio companies in this space...")
3. **Be wrong publicly**: When a thesis doesn't play out, write about why — this builds more trust than always being right
4. **Timeliness**: Market Pulse must go out within 48 hours of major market events (special editions)
5. **Engagement**: End every piece with a question or invitation for dialogue
6. **Consistency**: Never miss a scheduled publication — reliability is credibility
