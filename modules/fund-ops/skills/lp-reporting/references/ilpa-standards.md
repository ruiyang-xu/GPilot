# ILPA Reporting Standards Summary

The Institutional Limited Partners Association (ILPA) establishes best practices for GP-LP reporting.

## Quarterly Report Requirements

### Timing
- Quarterly reports within **60 days** of quarter-end
- Annual audited statements within **120 days** of fiscal year-end
- Capital call notices with **10 business days** advance notice (minimum)

### Required Content
1. **Fund-Level Information**
   - Total commitments, called capital, distributions, NAV
   - Performance metrics: IRR (gross and net), TVPI, DPI, RVPI
   - Management fees and expenses detail
   - Cash position and unfunded commitments

2. **Portfolio Company Information** (per company)
   - Company name, description, and sector
   - Investment date and cost basis
   - Current fair value and valuation methodology
   - MOIC
   - Key developments and milestones
   - Board representation status

3. **Capital Account Statement** (per LP)
   - Beginning balance
   - Contributions and distributions during period
   - Share of gains/losses
   - Fees and expenses allocated
   - Ending balance

4. **GP Letter**
   - Market commentary
   - Portfolio highlights and challenges
   - Deployment pace and pipeline
   - Outlook

## Performance Reporting Standards

### IRR Calculation
- **Gross IRR**: Cash flows to/from investments, before fees and carry
- **Net IRR**: Cash flows to/from LPs, after all fees, expenses, and carry
- Use actual dates of cash flows (not period-end)
- Include unrealized NAV as terminal cash flow

### Valuation Standards
- Follow ASC 820 / IPEV guidelines
- Document methodology for each investment
- Note when valuation is based on latest round vs. independent assessment
- Disclose any write-ups or write-downs with rationale

### Benchmarking
- Compare against relevant vintage year benchmarks (Cambridge Associates, Preqin)
- Note: benchmarks lag and methodology varies — present with appropriate caveats

## Fee Disclosure (ILPA Template)
- Management fee rate and base
- Organizational expenses (capped amount and usage)
- Fund-level operating expenses
- Transaction and monitoring fees (if any)
- Fee offsets applied
- Net management fee paid

## ESG Reporting (Encouraged)
- Portfolio company ESG practices
- Diversity metrics (fund and portfolio)
- Climate impact considerations
- Governance practices

## Best Practices
- Maintain consistent formatting quarter-over-quarter
- Provide both tabular data and narrative context
- Be transparent about underperformers — LPs will find out anyway
- Offer LP calls for questions after report distribution
- Track communication frequency per LP in lp-database.xlsx
