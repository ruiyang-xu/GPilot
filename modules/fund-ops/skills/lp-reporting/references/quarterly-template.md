# Quarterly Report — Structure & Style Guide

## Document Structure
See `templates/quarterly-report.md` for the full template. This guide adds GP-specific guidance.

## GP Letter — Writing Guide

### Tone
- Professional but personal — the GP is a solo GP, not a faceless institution
- Confident but not arrogant
- Honest about challenges — LPs value transparency above all
- Forward-looking — always include outlook

### Structure (1 page max)
1. **Opening**: Brief macro context (2-3 sentences) relevant to portfolio
2. **Highlights**: Top 2-3 positive developments
3. **Challenges**: Be upfront about any issues (1-2 sentences each)
4. **Deployment**: New investments, pipeline activity, pace
5. **Outlook**: What you're watching, what you expect
6. **Close**: Availability for questions

### What LPs Care About Most
1. **Performance numbers** — IRR and TVPI first, everything else second
2. **Capital deployment** — Are you investing the money? What's the pace?
3. **Portfolio company health** — Winners, losers, risks
4. **Follow-on strategy** — Are you supporting winners?
5. **Exits and DPI** — When do they get money back?

## Portfolio Company Updates

### Per Company (Aim for 1/2 page each)
- Lead with metrics (revenue, growth, key KPI)
- One paragraph on progress this quarter
- One sentence on outlook / next milestones
- Flag any risk factors

### Valuation Commentary
- Explain any material valuation changes (>20% up or down)
- Reference methodology: "Marked at last round", "Comparable company multiple", etc.
- Be conservative — LPs prefer upside surprises to markdowns

## Numbers Presentation
- Use consistent formatting: $1.2M (not $1,200,000 or $1.2 million)
- Round appropriately: $1.2M (not $1,234,567) for readability
- Show trends: "Revenue grew 40% QoQ to $2.1M"
- Always provide context: compare to plan, prior quarter, or benchmark
