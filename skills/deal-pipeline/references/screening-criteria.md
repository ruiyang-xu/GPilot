# Screening Criteria — Cross-Stage Tech-Focused Generalist

## Scoring Framework

Each factor scored 1-5. Composite = weighted sum. Threshold: 3.5+ → DD, 2.5-3.5 → Monitor, <2.5 → Pass.

---

## 1. Team (Weight: 30%)

| Score | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| 5 | Repeat founder with exits; deep domain expertise; complementary co-founders | Proven team that's scaled 0→1 before; strong VP-level hires | Executive team with public company or $100M+ scale experience |
| 4 | Strong domain background; prior startup experience; technical co-founder | Solid execution track record; key hires in place | Experienced C-suite; most functional leaders hired |
| 3 | Relevant industry experience; first-time founders with strong backgrounds | Capable team but gaps in key roles | Good team but missing key executives for next phase |
| 2 | Limited relevant experience; solo founder without complementary skills | High turnover; key roles unfilled after 12+ months | Executive gaps; founder struggling to delegate |
| 1 | No relevant domain experience; team red flags (integrity, commitment) | Dysfunctional team dynamics; founder-market mismatch | Leadership crisis; key departures |

**Red Flags**: Founder integrity concerns, inability to articulate vision, excessive founder compensation at early stage, co-founder disputes.

**Auto-Elevate**: YC/top accelerator alumni (+0.5), prior successful exit (+1.0), deep technical PhD in relevant field (+0.5).

---

## 2. Market (Weight: 25%)

| Score | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| 5 | TAM >$10B; clear secular tailwind; timing is perfect | TAM >$10B with clear SAM >$1B; regulatory/structural moat | Category-defining opportunity; winner-take-most dynamics |
| 4 | TAM >$5B; growing market; favorable trends | Large addressable market with clear go-to-market path | Strong market position; expanding TAM |
| 3 | TAM >$1B; reasonable growth assumptions | Decent market but crowded or unclear differentiation path | Competitive market; position defensible but not dominant |
| 2 | TAM <$1B or highly speculative sizing | Market smaller than pitched; strong incumbents | Market maturing; growth slowing |
| 1 | Niche market with no expansion path | Declining market or regulatory headwinds | Commoditized market; race to bottom |

**Key Questions**: Is this a vitamin or painkiller? What's the catalyst for "why now"? How do incumbents respond?

---

## 3. Product (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| 5 | Breakthrough technology; strong IP; working prototype with early signal | Clear PMF; users love it (NPS >50); technical moat | Best-in-class product; strong platform/network effects |
| 4 | Innovative approach; MVP with promising early data | Good product-market fit evidence; improving metrics | Strong product with defensible advantages |
| 3 | Solid concept; MVP in development or early testing | Product works but undifferentiated; some PMF signals | Good product but limited moat; feature competition risk |
| 2 | Me-too product; unclear technical advantage | Product issues; churn concerns; weak differentiation | Losing product edge; competitors catching up |
| 1 | No working product; pure concept | Product not working; pivoting; users churning | Product obsolescence risk; tech debt overwhelming |

**Tech-Focused Lens**: Prioritize companies with genuine technical differentiation — proprietary algorithms, novel architectures, unique data assets, or hard-to-replicate engineering.

---

## 4. Traction (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| 5 | Exceptional early signal: waitlist >10K, LOIs from enterprises, viral growth | >3x YoY revenue growth; NDR >130%; top-decile engagement | >$10M ARR growing >100%; strong unit economics |
| 4 | Good early traction: paying customers, strong engagement metrics | 2-3x YoY growth; healthy retention; expanding accounts | $5-10M ARR; 50-100% growth; improving margins |
| 3 | Some traction: beta users, pilot customers, letter of intent | Revenue growing but <2x; acceptable retention | Moderate growth; unit economics breakeven |
| 2 | Minimal traction: few users, no revenue signal | Flat or decelerating growth; retention concerns | Growth stalling; CAC rising; churn increasing |
| 1 | No traction; pre-product | Revenue declining; users leaving | Declining revenue; negative unit economics |

**Stage-Adjusted**: Seed companies can score 4-5 with strong qualitative signals (expert validation, design partnerships) even without revenue.

---

## 5. Thesis Fit (Weight: 10%)

| Score | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| 5 | Core sector (AI/ML, SaaS, fintech, healthtech, deeptech); unique angle | Strong portfolio synergies; co-investment potential | Strategic platform investment; ecosystem value |
| 4 | Adjacent tech sector; compelling tech angle | Good fit with portfolio; reasonable synergies | Solid addition to portfolio; diversification value |
| 3 | Tech-enabled but not core tech; acceptable sector | Limited synergies but standalone merit | Acceptable fit; no conflicts |
| 2 | Non-tech sector with tech wrapper | Potential portfolio conflicts; limited strategic value | Concentration risk; limited strategic fit |
| 1 | Outside mandate entirely | Direct conflict with existing portfolio | No strategic rationale |

---

## Composite Score Interpretation

| Score Range | Action | Expected Frequency |
|-------------|--------|--------------------|
| 4.5 - 5.0 | Fast-track to DD; high conviction | ~5% of pipeline |
| 3.5 - 4.4 | Proceed to DD; standard process | ~15% of pipeline |
| 2.5 - 3.4 | Monitor; request more info; revisit in 3-6 months | ~30% of pipeline |
| 1.5 - 2.4 | Pass with rationale logged | ~35% of pipeline |
| 1.0 - 1.4 | Quick pass; outside mandate | ~15% of pipeline |
