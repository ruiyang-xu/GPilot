# Term Sheet Guide — Standard Terms by Round

## Economic Terms

### Valuation
| Stage | Typical Pre-Money | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Pre-seed | $3M-$10M | Often via SAFE/convertible note |
| Seed | $8M-$20M | SAFE or priced round |
| Series A | $20M-$60M | Priced round with full governance |
| Series B | $50M-$150M | Growth metrics-driven |
| Growth | $150M+ | Revenue multiple-driven |

### Liquidation Preference
- **Standard**: 1x non-participating preferred (most founder-friendly)
- **Acceptable**: 1x participating with 3x cap
- **Red Flag**: >1x preference, uncapped participation, senior liquidation preference stacking

### Anti-Dilution
- **Standard**: Broad-based weighted average
- **Red Flag**: Full ratchet (heavily dilutive to founders in down round)

### Dividends
- **Standard**: Non-cumulative, as declared by board
- **Caution**: Cumulative dividends (acts as extra liquidation preference over time)

## Control Terms

### Board Composition
| Stage | Typical |
|-------|---------|
| Seed | 2 founders + 1 investor or 3 founders |
| Series A | 2 founders + 1 lead investor + 1 independent |
| Series B+ | 2 founders + 2 investors + 1 independent |

### Protective Provisions (Standard)
Investor consent typically required for:
- Issuing new equity or debt above threshold
- Changing authorized shares
- Amending charter/bylaws
- Declaring dividends or redemptions
- Selling the company or major assets
- Changing board size

### Voting Rights
- **Standard**: 1 share = 1 vote (common and preferred)
- **Watch**: Dual-class structures, super-voting shares

---

## Investor Rights

### Information Rights
- Monthly financials (Series A+)
- Annual audited statements
- Annual budget and business plan
- Cap table updates

### Pro-Rata Rights
- **Standard**: Right to maintain ownership in future rounds
- **Our Position**: Always request pro-rata; critical for follow-on strategy

### Registration Rights
- **Standard**: Demand registration (2x), S-3 registration, piggyback rights
- Typically not exercised in practice for venture rounds

### ROFR and Co-Sale
- **ROFR**: Company and/or investors have right of first refusal on secondary sales
- **Co-Sale**: Investors can participate pro-rata in founder secondary sales
- **Standard**: Both are typical and acceptable

### Drag-Along
- **Standard**: Majority of preferred + majority of common can force sale
- **Watch**: Low threshold drag-along; ensure fair value floor

---

## Other Key Terms

### Option Pool
- **Standard**: 10-15% unissued pool (post-money) at Series A
- **Watch**: Excessive pool increase that dilutes existing holders
- **Our Position**: Pool should reflect actual 18-24 month hiring plan

### Founder Vesting
- **Standard**: 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff, with acceleration on change of control
- **Watch**: No vesting on existing shares; immediate full vesting

### No-Shop / Exclusivity
- **Standard**: 30-45 days exclusivity after term sheet signing
- **Watch**: >60 days exclusivity without binding commitment

### Pay-to-Play
- **Red Flag for Us**: Forces participation in down rounds or lose rights
- **Our Position**: Resist; prefer voluntary participation decisions

---

## GP Checklist Before Signing

1. [ ] Valuation supported by comps and return analysis
2. [ ] 1x non-participating preferred (or understand why not)
3. [ ] Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
4. [ ] Board seat or observer rights secured
5. [ ] Pro-rata rights for follow-on
6. [ ] Standard information rights
7. [ ] Reasonable protective provisions
8. [ ] Option pool sized to actual hiring plan
9. [ ] No unusual drag-along or redemption provisions
10. [ ] Side letter needs identified (MFN, co-invest, reporting)
