# Editorial Checklist — Quality Gate

The `editor` agent uses this checklist to review every research draft. Issues are categorized as:
- **Critical** (C): Must fix before publication. Factual errors, logical flaws, quality below bar.
- **Important** (I): Should fix. Strengthens the piece significantly.
- **Suggestion** (S): Optional improvement. Nice to have.

---

## 1. Thesis & Structure

- [ ] **(C)** Is the main argument stated clearly in the first paragraph / executive summary?
- [ ] **(C)** Does the piece have a clear "so what?" — why should the reader care?
- [ ] **(I)** Does the structure follow the appropriate template?
- [ ] **(I)** Is there a logical flow? Does each section build on the previous one?
- [ ] **(S)** Is the opening hook compelling? Would you keep reading after the first 3 sentences?

## 2. Original Thinking

- [ ] **(C)** Does this contain at least one original framework, insight, or non-consensus view?
- [ ] **(C)** Is this more than a summary of publicly available information?
- [ ] **(I)** Would a reader get something from this they couldn't get from a Google search?
- [ ] **(I)** Are the GP's unique perspectives (portfolio experience, cross-stage view) leveraged?
- [ ] **(S)** Is there a proprietary framework that could be branded and reused?

## 3. Data Integrity

- [ ] **(C)** Are all numbers sourced? Can each statistic be traced to a credible source?
- [ ] **(C)** Are sources less than 12 months old (preferably <6 months)?
- [ ] **(C)** Are there any numbers that seem wrong or inconsistent with each other?
- [ ] **(I)** Is the data presented in context? (YoY change, vs. benchmark, vs. peers)
- [ ] **(S)** Could any claims be strengthened with additional data?

## 4. Analytical Rigor

- [ ] **(C)** Are there unsupported logical leaps? ("AI is growing fast, therefore Company X will succeed")
- [ ] **(C)** Are counterarguments addressed? (Bull/bear balance)
- [ ] **(I)** Is the analysis at the right level — specific enough to be useful, but not so narrow as to miss the big picture?
- [ ] **(I)** Are assumptions stated explicitly?
- [ ] **(S)** Could scenario analysis strengthen the argument?

## 5. Competitive Quality

- [ ] **(C)** Would this hold up against a Goldman Sachs / AB / LatePost piece on the same topic?
- [ ] **(I)** Is the depth of analysis appropriate for the publication type?
- [ ] **(I)** Are the visuals (charts, tables, frameworks) publication-quality?
- [ ] **(S)** Would you share this on your own LinkedIn if you received it?

## 6. Actionability

- [ ] **(C)** Does the reader know what to do with this information?
- [ ] **(I)** Are investment implications explicit (for investor audience)?
- [ ] **(I)** Are forward-looking signposts identified? ("Watch for X as a signal that Y")
- [ ] **(S)** Is there a clear "the GP's Take" or recommendation section?

## 7. Writing Quality

- [ ] **(C)** Is the writing clear and free of ambiguity?
- [ ] **(I)** Is the tone appropriate for the publication type and audience?
- [ ] **(I)** Are there redundant sections or paragraphs that can be cut?
- [ ] **(S)** Does the piece have good rhythm? (Varying sentence/paragraph length)
- [ ] **(S)** Is technical jargon explained on first use?

## 8. Visual Quality

- [ ] **(I)** Are charts/tables clear, accurate, and properly labeled?
- [ ] **(I)** Does every visual earn its place? (Remove decorative-only elements)
- [ ] **(S)** Are visuals consistent in style (color palette, fonts)?
- [ ] **(S)** Would the visuals be readable on a mobile phone screen?

## 9. Bilingual Consistency (for CN version review)

- [ ] **(C)** Does the Chinese version convey the same analytical substance as the English?
- [ ] **(C)** Does the Chinese read as native-quality writing, not translated text?
- [ ] **(I)** Are technical terms using standard Chinese financial conventions?
- [ ] **(I)** Is the tone calibrated for Chinese readership (more narrative, less bullet-point)?
- [ ] **(S)** Are English terms parenthesized correctly on first use?

## 10. Brand Alignment

- [ ] **(I)** Does this reflect the GP's voice and the fund's positioning?
- [ ] **(I)** Is the fund properly branded (header, footer, disclaimer)?
- [ ] **(S)** Does this piece build the fund's reputation in its focus areas?
- [ ] **(S)** Would LPs be proud to share this with their networks?

---

## Review Output Format

```markdown
## Editorial Review — {Title}
**Type**: {Publication type}
**Reviewer**: Editor Agent
**Date**: {date}
**Overall Assessment**: Ready / Needs Revision / Major Rewrite

### Critical Issues ({count})
{Each with specific location and suggested fix}

### Important Issues ({count})
{Each with specific location and suggested fix}

### Suggestions ({count})
{Optional improvements}

### Strengths
{2-3 things the piece does well — calibrate for balanced feedback}
```
