# Writing Style Guide — Bilingual Research Platform

## English Writing Standards

### Goldman Sachs Benchmark (Sector Reports)
- **Lead with the investment implication**, not the description. First paragraph answers "why should I care?"
- **Proprietary frameworks**: Create branded analytical tools (e.g., "AI Adoption Maturity Index", "Vertical SaaS Scoring Matrix"). Every major piece should introduce one.
- **Data density**: Tables and charts carry the argument. Prose provides interpretation, not redundant description of what the chart shows.
- **"So what?" test**: Every section must answer what this means for investors. If it's just information without implication, cut it.
- **Quantitative precision**: "$4.2B TAM growing at 23% CAGR" not "a large and growing market"
- **Actionable conclusions**: End with specific, ranked investment themes or recommendations

### AllianceBernstein Benchmark (Thematic Research)
- **Contrarian framing**: "The consensus says X. We believe Y, because..." This structure immediately signals original thinking.
- **Macro-to-micro bridge**: Start with the big picture, then zoom to specific company-level evidence. Never stay at one altitude.
- **Quantitative backing for qualitative claims**: "Switching costs are high" → "Switching costs average 18 months of revenue, based on our analysis of 12 vertical SaaS transitions"
- **Scenario thinking**: Present bull/base/bear explicitly. Show your work on assumptions.
- **Time horizons**: Be specific. "In the next 2-3 years" not "going forward"

### English Tone
- Authoritative but accessible
- Not academic (no jargon for jargon's sake)
- Not journalistic (not chasing clicks)
- Analytical: the reader should feel smarter after reading
- Concise: every sentence earns its place

## Chinese Writing Standards (中文写作标准)

### 晚点财经 Benchmark (Narrative Deep Dives)
- **以场景开头**: Open with a vivid scene, key moment, or striking detail that captures the thesis. Not "本文将讨论..."
- **叙事驱动**: Weave data into storytelling. Numbers support narrative, not replace it.
- **降低理解门槛**: Make complex business models feel intuitive through analogies and progressive explanation
- **内部视角感**: Write as if the reader is getting insider understanding, not reading a press release
- **节奏感**: Vary paragraph length. Short punchy paragraphs after dense analytical sections. Create rhythm.

### 海外独角兽 Benchmark (Company Teardowns)
- **信息差弥合**: Bridge the gap between Chinese readers and overseas tech/business world
- **类比思维**: Explain Western concepts with Chinese market analogues ("X is the Meituan of Y")
- **框架先行**: Present analytical frameworks Chinese investors already understand, then apply to the subject
- **创始人故事**: Chinese readers value founder narratives — include the human story behind the business
- **兼顾深度与可读性**: Deep enough for professional investors, accessible enough for curious operators

### Chinese Tone (中文调性)
- 专业但不枯燥 — Professional but not dry
- 有观点 — Opinionated, backed by evidence
- 有洞察 — Insightful, not just informational
- 有温度 — Has warmth and personality, especially in GP Letters
- 不翻译腔 — Must NOT read like translated English

### Chinese Financial Terminology
Use standard Chinese financial writing conventions:
| English | Chinese | NOT |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Valuation | 估值 | 定价/价值评估 |
| Moat | 护城河 | 壕沟 |
| Product-Market Fit | PMF / 产品市场契合 | 产品适合市场 |
| Fundraising | 融资 | 募资 (unless LP context) |
| LP fundraising | 募资 | 融资 |
| Due diligence | 尽调 / 尽职调查 | 应有注意 |
| Exit | 退出 | 离开 |
| Burn rate | 烧钱速度/消耗速度 | 燃烧率 |
| Runway | 现金跑道 | 跑道 |
| ARR | ARR (年度经常性收入) | 年收入 |
| TAM | TAM (潜在市场规模) | 总可寻址市场 |
| Series A/B/C | A轮/B轮/C轮 | 系列A |

### Bilingual Technical Terms Protocol
First mention: Chinese + English parenthetical → 大语言模型 (Large Language Model, LLM)
Subsequent mentions: Use the shorter form → LLM or 大模型

## the GP's Voice (GP Letters)

### English
- First person, thoughtful, slightly contrarian
- Draws from real experience (anonymized portfolio insights)
- One big idea per letter — not a laundry list
- Opens with an observation or question, not a greeting
- Comfortable with uncertainty ("I don't know the answer yet, but here's how I'm thinking about it")

### Chinese (彼得的声音)
- 第一人称，娓娓道来
- 引用真实投资经验（匿名化）
- 每封信一个核心观点
- 有个人温度，不是机构口吻
- 可以表达不确定性，展现思考过程

## Universal Principles (Both Languages)

1. **Original > Aggregated**: Never just summarize what others have written. Add analytical value.
2. **Data > Opinion**: Back claims with numbers. "We believe" is weak; "The data shows" is strong.
3. **Specific > Vague**: "Q4 2025 revenue was $12.3M, up 47% YoY" beats "strong recent growth"
4. **Structure > Stream of consciousness**: Use headers, tables, and frameworks. Readers scan first, then read.
5. **Honest > Promotional**: Acknowledge risks and counterarguments. Credibility is the only asset.
6. **Concise > Comprehensive**: Better to be deep on 3 points than shallow on 10.
